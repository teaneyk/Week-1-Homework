The Triple Bypass


For some people the term Triple Bypass means to undergo a serious surgery to repair damage to their heart. For other people it can mean a serious bike ride that will attempt to blow up their heart.

The Triple Bypass is a 110 mile route that travels from Evergreen to Avon CO over Juniper Pass (11,140 ft.), Loveland Pass (11,990 ft.), Swan Mountain and Vail Pass (10,560 ft>)


The ride includes four fully stocked aid stations, a lunch served at Loveland aid station, free beers and dinner at the finish in Avon. It also includes a Triple Bypass Jersey and a finisher medal.
